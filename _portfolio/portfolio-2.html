Simulating Safety: Predicting Sarin Gas Dispersion in Urban Environments with CFD Precision.

Introduction

The leakage of sarin gas in an urban environment can be harmful. This project investigates the dispersion of sarin gas using Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) to solve turbulence models and visualize the gas spread.

CFD Simulations

The CFD equations were solved using the finite difference method in ANSYS Fluent. The simulation incorporated:

  • Flow Model: Steady-state, incompressible flow, 3D
  • Equations Solved: Navier-Stokes, Energy, and Continuity equations
  • Turbulence Model: Realizable k-ϵ model
  • Algorithm: SIMPLEC for efficient computation

Mesh Details

Mesh Specifications

  • Total Elements: 0.35 million
  • Minimum Mesh Quality: 0.44
  • Discretization Tool: ICEM

Simulation Parameters

Wind velocity: 3 m/s; Sarin release velocity: 150 m/s. A multiphase mixture flow model was considered with air and sarin gas phases.

Results

Contamination Analysis

Contamination was studied at various altitudes:

  • Ground Level (0m): Moderate spread
  • Low Altitude (3m): Increasing concentration
  • Mid Altitude (5m): Maximum spread observed
  • High Altitude (10m, 15m): Decreasing concentration pattern

Conclusion

The study concluded that the sarin gas spread is limited under the simulated conditions, though proximity to the contamination zone poses risks. These findings contribute to understanding urban contamination patterns and can aid in developing safety protocols.
