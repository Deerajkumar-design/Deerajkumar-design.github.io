Agatsya: Development of a Sounding Rocket

Project Overview

Agatsya is a high-performance sounding rocket designed and developed by Team Sammard for the prestigious Spaceport America Cup. The project reflects the team's dedication to innovation and technical expertise, aiming to achieve precision flight mechanics and scientific exploration. Agatsya stands as a testament to advanced aerodynamics, robust propulsion systems, and meticulous engineering.

Key Objectives

  1. Design and Launch: Develop a rocket capable of reaching specified altitudes with maximum stability and safety.
  2. Scientific Payload Delivery: Integrate a versatile payload for experimental research.
  3. Data Acquisition: Use onboard avionics for telemetry, altitude tracking, and aerodynamic analysis.
  4. Innovation and Teamwork: Foster a collaborative approach to solve real-world engineering challenges.

Design and Engineering Highlights

1. CAD Representation and Motor Analysis

Agatsya is powered by an M-Class 8212M1630-P-type commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) solid rocket motor. The motor's thrust-time curve was carefully analyzed to optimize flight performance, ensuring reliability and efficiency. CAD modeling allowed precise geometrical design and seamless integration of components.

2. Aerodynamics and Stability

  • Nose Cone: A Von Karman curve profile was selected using OpenRocket software, balancing drag reduction and structural integrity.
  • CFD Analysis: Computational Fluid Dynamics was employed to analyze pressure distribution across the rocket's surface, enabling aerodynamic refinement.
  • Swept-Back Fins: Tailored dimensions and profiles were engineered to ensure in-flight stability and minimize aerodynamic disturbances.

3. Flight Mechanics

Agatsya demonstrated excellent flight stability with its trajectory and velocity optimized for the launch conditions. Key flight events were tracked to validate the design's accuracy.

Scientific Payload Integration

PiMAC Payload System

The payload, PiMAC, includes:

  • Electronics: Advanced PCB design, gimbal-mounted servo motors, and microcontrollers ensure real-time adjustments.
  • Sensors: Integrated IMUs (Inertial Measurement Units) and PWM controllers for precise control.
  • Control System: A robust transfer function and control loop governed payload dynamics, allowing smooth operation and data acquisition during flight.

Avionics and Telemetry

The avionics system was a pivotal component of Agatsya:

  • Altimeters: Equipped with Featherweight Raven 4 and Missile Works RRC3 altimeters for accurate apogee and parachute deployment tracking.
  • GPS Tracking: Featherweight GPS tracked flight trajectory and estimated drift distance for recovery.
  • Parachute Deployment: A carefully engineered parachute design ensured safe descent and recovery.

Innovations

  1. Control System Optimization: Designed for minimal response delays under various flight conditions.
  2. Advanced Avionics: Integrated multiple telemetry systems to maximize flight data accuracy.
  3. Custom Payload: PiMAC offered a unique approach to scientific exploration, combining mechanical, electrical, and software innovations.

Achievements

Agatsya demonstrated outstanding performance at the Spaceport America Cup, achieving a top rank among Asia-Pacific teams. The project highlighted the potential for young engineers to create reliable and innovative aerospace solutions.
